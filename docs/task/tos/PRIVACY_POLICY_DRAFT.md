# PartnerUp Privacy Policy (Draft)

**Version:** `2026-03-18`  
**Effective Date:** `March 18, 2026`

## 1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how PartnerUp collects, uses, stores, and protects personal information when you use the service.

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Information You Provide

1. Profile information (for example, nickname, avatar).
2. PartnerRequest content and related activity input.
3. Support/contact messages.

### 2.2 Information Generated During Use

1. Account identifiers (for example, `user_id`).
2. Credential-related data (for example, hashed PIN, access token metadata).
3. Participation and interaction records (for example, create, publish, join, exit, confirm, check-in).
4. Notification settings and subscription preferences.

### 2.3 WeChat and Third-Party Identity Data

When applicable, PartnerUp may process WeChat OAuth data such as `openid` and profile fields obtained via authorized flows.

### 2.4 Device and Technical Data

1. Browser/session data needed for security and session restore.
2. Local storage keys used for product operation (for example, `user_id`, `user_pin`, token, session role, consent state).
3. Basic analytics or attribution data (for example, route, event, `spm` parameters).

## 3. How We Use Information

We use personal information to:

1. Provide and maintain service functionality.
2. Authenticate users and secure accounts.
3. Support PR lifecycle actions and collaboration workflows.
4. Send reminders and service-related notices when enabled.
5. Detect abuse, prevent fraud, and improve reliability.
6. Analyze product usage and improve user experience.
7. Comply with legal obligations.

## 4. Legal Basis and Consent

Depending on the jurisdiction and scenario, processing may be based on:

1. Your consent.
2. Contract/service necessity.
3. Legitimate interest (for security, stability, and product improvement).
4. Legal compliance obligations.

Where consent is required, you may withdraw it subject to legal and technical limitations.

## 5. Sharing and Disclosure

PartnerUp does not sell personal information.

We may share information only when necessary, such as:

1. Service providers supporting hosting, storage, security, or messaging.
2. Legal, regulatory, or law-enforcement requests where required by law.
3. Business restructuring scenarios (for example, merger or asset transfer), with appropriate safeguards.

## 6. Data Retention

1. We retain data only for as long as needed for the purposes described here, unless longer retention is required by law.
2. Retention periods may differ by data category (account, operations, logs, compliance records).
3. When data is no longer required, we delete or anonymize it, subject to legal constraints.

## 7. Data Security

PartnerUp applies reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal information, including access control and secure storage practices.

No system is absolutely secure. You should also protect your own credentials and device environment.

## 8. Cross-Border Processing

If cross-border processing is involved, PartnerUp will apply safeguards required by applicable law.

## 9. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you may have rights to:

1. Access your personal information.
2. Correct inaccurate information.
3. Request deletion in eligible cases.
4. Withdraw consent where consent is the legal basis.
5. Request explanation regarding processing rules.

You may submit requests through in-product support/contact channels.

## 10. Children

If required by applicable law, minors should use the service only with guardian consent.

## 11. Policy Updates

PartnerUp may update this Privacy Policy.

When material updates occur:

1. A new version and effective date will be published.
2. Renewed agreement may be required before continuing authenticated usage.

## 12. Contact

For privacy-related questions or requests, please contact PartnerUp through the in-product support/contact channel.

## 13. Draft Notice

This document is a product/legal draft for implementation and internal review.  
Final legal text should be reviewed by qualified legal counsel before production release.
